Saint John Neumann

'The Little Bishop'

icon of Saint John Neumann, C.S.S.R

Saint John Neumann was a Redemptorist Catholic priest and the fourth Bishop of the Diocese of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He emigrated to the United States from his native Bohemia, now the Czech Republic, and became a citizen and is the first male American saint in the Catholic Church.

Known informally as 'the Little Bishop' due to his small stature, Saint John Neumann is widely recognized as the founder of the American parochial school system. Under his administration, the number of diocesean schools jumped from one to 200, and enrollment lept from 500 to 9,000 students in just two years.
